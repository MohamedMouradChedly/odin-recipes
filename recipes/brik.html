Tunisian Brik is a savory pastry that represents a delightful blend of textures and flavors, with the runny egg yolk adding a luscious element to the dish. Brik is often served with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice and a dash of harissa sauce for a spicy kick. It's a popular snack or appetizer in Tunisia, loved for its delicious taste and satisfying crunch.

Ingredients:

  • 100 grams of preserved tuna
  • 50 grams of cilantro
  • 50 grams of onion
  • 4 eggs
  • 4 sheets of filo pastry
  • Oil for frying the brik
  • Lemon
  • Black pepper and salt

Steps:

  1. Finely chop the cilantro and onion.
  2. Break up some of the preserved tuna in oil, or you can cut it into small pieces.
  3. Fold the filo pastry sheets into squares. Each sheet should be divided into four equal squares.
  4. Place a quarter of the preserved tuna in the center of each filo pastry square, along with a little cilantro and onion.
  5. Carefully crack an egg over the mixture in the center of each pastry square.
  6. Sprinkle a pinch of black pepper and a pinch of salt over each egg.
  7. Carefully fold the filo pastry square to create a triangle or rectangle, enclosing the filling.
  8. Heat oil in a deep frying pan to about 350°F (175°C).
  9. Gently slide the folded brik into the hot oil. Fry until it turns golden brown, usually about 2-3 minutes on each side.
  10. Remove the brik from the oil and let it drain on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  11. Serve the brik hot, with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice for added flavor.
